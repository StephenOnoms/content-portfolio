Pricing FAQ

Why flat-rate instead of per-token pricing?

Per-token pricing means your bill changes every time you change models, and can be hard to predict when traffic is variable. Flat-rate pricing means your bill depends on how much concurrent capacity you need — not which model, or how verbose your prompts are. For teams running multiple models or experimenting frequently, this removes a recurring source of budget surprises.

What does "concurrent requests" mean for my bill?

It's the number of requests your account can have in progress at the same time. If your traffic is mostly steady with occasional bursts, size your plan to your typical peak — not your average. Your dashboard shows your concurrency usage over time, so you can see whether you're near your tier's limit.

What happens if I exceed my concurrency limit?

Requests beyond your tier's concurrent limit are queued briefly rather than rejected outright, so short bursts are smoothed out automatically. If you're consistently hitting your limit, your dashboard will flag it — that's usually a sign it's time to move up a tier.

Is this cheaper than per-token pricing?

It depends on your traffic. For steady, moderate-to-high traffic across multiple models, flat-rate is usually cheaper and more predictable. For very low-volume or highly sporadic traffic, a per-token provider may cost less in absolute terms — though often with less predictability. If you're unsure, the Developer tier is free, so you can compare your real usage against a per-token estimate before committing.

Can I change plans later?

Yes — you can move between tiers at any time from your dashboard. Changes take effect at the start of your next billing cycle, so there's no penalty for trying a higher tier during a traffic spike and stepping back down afterward.
