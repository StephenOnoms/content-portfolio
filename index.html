I've spent the last five years writing the content that gets B2B SaaS products found, understood, and chosen — 100+ published articles, double-digit lifts in organic traffic and qualified leads, and a working method for turning a feature nobody's heard of into a page that ranks and converts.

I'm now applying that method to a harder and more interesting problem: AI infrastructure and developer tooling, where the writing has to be technically accurate, useful to an engineer at 11pm, and still good enough to rank. I'm backing this with an MSc in Digital Marketing & Analytics, so the content decisions I make are increasingly grounded in data, not instinct.

This portfolio is the result: explainers on LLM inference and pricing, landing page and documentation copy written for a developer audience, and the content calendars I'd build to plan it all. Everything here is written the way I'd write on the job — structured, direct, and built to survive an editor's red pen.
